3 · The finding

Findings

What you signed up for: the AutoScientist Challenge, a four-week open competition from Adaption Labs (the startup founded by ex-Cohere leaders Sara Hooker and Sudip Roy). The “You’re In” email is just onboarding collateral; the actual rules live on the challenge page.

How a submission works — four parts:

  1. Pick one of ten categories.
  2. Open-source your adapted finetuning dataset and trained weights to both Hugging Face and Kaggle, crediting Adaptive Data by Adaption.
  3. Show a measurable % improvement over a baseline on Adaption’s held-out, in-house test set (they grade you on data you never see).
  4. (Bonus) demos + social posts tagging @adaption_ai / adaption-labs.

Prizes: $50,000 total — 10×$4,000 firsts (one per category), 10×$1,000 runners-up, 30 honorary mentions. Adaption provides ~$1,000 in adaptation credits + free compute, a Discord, and research-team office hours.

The strategic read: because prizes are awarded per category, you only need to be best in one. So the highest-leverage move is category selection, not out-muscling a crowded field. The full analysis is in FINDINGS.md; the strategy track is in todo.md.
