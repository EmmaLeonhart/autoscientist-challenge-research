FAQ
We will update this FAQ Section as the challenge progresses.
Hello everyone excited to work on the challenge and use autoscientist, just wanted to ask how many collaborators are allowed per team?
There is not a limit on collaborators per team. There needs to be a designated team captain. If the group wins, the team captain will be sent the prize and be in charge of distribution.
Participation & Eligibility
1. Can I do both parts? Yes — you can enter Part 1 and Part 2 and be eligible for prizes in both. Winners for Part 1 are announced July 13, then Part 2 kicks off July 6.
2. Can I submit to multiple tracks? Yes. Prizes are awarded per track, so each submission is evaluated independently.
Compute & Credits
3. I signed up but haven't received compute credits. The 50 credits in your account are from the free tier, not the challenge. Reach out in #support on Discord and the team will sort it.
Evaluation & Scoring
4. What's the baseline model? Your model is evaluated relative to the baseline you train on, not a fixed external model. Dataset quality and held-out test set performance both factor into scoring.
5. Do we see the held-out test sets? No — they're internal. Language track uses LLM-as-judge win rates against a base model on unseen tasks, scored on language competence. Other tracks follow the same principle.
6. Language track — what tasks specifically? Optimize broadly for language quality rather than a single task. The judge scores on overall competence, not a specific benchmark.
7. Healthcare track — can we report extra metrics? Yes, and it's encouraged. Additional clinical task metrics will be looked on favorably by judges.
Data & Licensing
8. Is CC-BY-NC data acceptable? Yes. Any open license that permits public release and attribution works.
Deadlines
9. Blog says July 5, terms page says June 22 — which is right? July 5 is correct. The terms page is being updated. Blog and Discord announcements take precedence.
